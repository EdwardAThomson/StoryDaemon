# Dev Log

## 2026-07-11

Recorded descent run 4, the sharpest diagnosis yet of why the finale keeps overshooting its calm target. This run existed to observe the new slot-aligned batch cap, but the cap never fired: a beat's `char_in_prose C003` postcondition failed seven consecutive ticks, pinned the queue, and forced the finale to re-execute a wedged rising-phase beat (8 vs target 4). The revealing part is that the beat's *event* was on the page repeatedly, the check failed only on *who*, because the writer is shown the raw ID (`C003`) rather than the character's name while the story minted nine new named security characters. So a satisfied beat was unverifiable by construction: a grounded-identity gap on the contract surface, not the writer defying the plan. Also logged: the staleness threshold missed the finale slot by arithmetic (2.8 vs step 3) for the second run running, and the first loop closure ever observed across the whole program (79 opened, 1 closed).

**Decisions & notes:** The `char_in_prose`-by-ID gap is the actionable takeaway: contract checkers that read prose need the character's grounded name, not the canonical ID, to match what the writer was actually given. Loop closure remains essentially nonexistent, continuing to point at loop-aging as the next real pressure to build.

## 2026-07-10

A large single-day burst, driven end to end by a sequence of plot-first "descent" validation runs (aiming the arc at a calm resolution and watching where it breaks). Three runs' worth of findings turned into concrete fixes. From the contracts smoke run: a stochastic malformed-JSON response was poisoning the beat horizon by dropping into a line fallback that turned raw JSON source lines into persistent beat descriptions (now retries once and rejects code-fragment "beats"), and LLM contract authoring had never actually fired because the prompt's authoritative shape block omitted `postconditions`, so a schema-obedient model always omitted them. From the descent run proper, three more defects: the beat-generation token budget was hardcoded at 1000 and truncated every contracts-plus-arc-guidance batch (new `generation.beat_max_tokens`, default 2000), two checkers that can never pass today (`char_at_location`, `loop_resolved`) were steering the prompt toward wedging beats on impossible conditions (gated out of authoring, still registered for evaluation), and contract verification was moved to tick step 11.5 so state-reading checks see the post-scene world. Two further backstops followed from the clean re-run: `TENSION_FLOOR_CAP = 7` clamps any `tension_at_least` floor above the scorer's practical ceiling (an unsatisfiable floor had pinned the whole resolution window), and a beat that has gone stale off the curve no longer suppresses the schedule's guidance, so a stale peak can't hijack the finale. Finally, slot alignment: batches now cap to the remaining story runway (`min(count, L - T)`) so the last authored beat lands on the final tick and inherits the curve endpoint, with an explicit no-new-threats final-scene clause — fixing the arbiter run's denouement-scheduled-past-the-end bug.

Alongside the fixes, two larger threads. First, an empirical **block decomposition study**: paragraph-level structural annotation of six public-domain masters (Buchan, Eddison, Conrad, Austen, Haggard, Dunsany, ~970 paragraphs) against the generated novels, cross-validated across models (kappa 0.75, 8/8 blind probes). The headline conclusions matured over the day: block *proportions* are not the gap (generated prose sits inside the six-master distribution spread, and bulk distribution is confirmed useless as a gauge), the gap is *rhythm and commitment* — masters sustain long carrier set-pieces (565–1618 word runs vs 204–346) and exit interiority instead of looping it (master self-transition 14% vs generated 40%). The load-bearing DSL constraint that falls out is the interiority-exit rule. Second, a new **thread-interleaving design**: since the fork experiment proved the tension overshoot lives in the *assigned event*, not the writer prompt (pruning 80% of the writer prompt moved mean tension by 0.0), the tension curve is reframed as a scene-selection policy over a portfolio of threads — calm pages come from cutting away with a cliffhanger, not from becalming a hot event. The falsified prompt-surgery roadmap item was retired in favor of this.

Also shipped: **OpenRouter support** in the `api` backend (own OpenAI-compatible client singleton, `OPENROUTER_API_KEY`/`OPENROUTER_MODEL`, first test coverage for `multi_provider_llm`, live-validated on an 8-tick run). ROADMAP/CLAUDE.md/EMERGENT_COHERENCE_PLAN synced with the whole burst.

**Decisions & notes:** The two never-satisfiable checkers stay *registered in the evaluation engine* but are gated out of *authoring* — the vocabulary the prompt can draw from is deliberately narrower than what the engine can score, so beats can't be authored against conditions nothing in the pipeline can yet make true. The "4.5x-closer" proportions claim from the first pass was explicitly retired once the corpus grew to six masters — a good example of an early headline dissolving under more data. The wedged-beat problem is left deliberately un-auto-fixed (give-up/skip rules risk drifting off-outline); the elimination chain across the descent runs now points squarely at thread interleaving plus resolution sequencing as the real fix, not more prompt or rewrite pressure.

## 2026-07-09

Attacked the one failure the June validation runs kept showing: arc-pressure tracks rising tension targets but cannot de-escalate for a resolution, because the planner keeps choosing tense events no matter what number it is aimed at. The fix is the arc-phase planner mandate (Phase 3): the target tension curve is now also read as a story phase (rising, peak, falling, resolution), and the planner gets a firm event-level mandate per phase (escalate, confront, resolve) instead of only a numeric target. The mandate covers both the Stage 1 strategic path and plot-first's `plan_for_beat` (injected into the Stage 3 tactical prompt, suppressed when the beat carries its own `tension_target`). The bounded tension rewrite is now skipped when the target drop is large enough that different events, not different prose, are needed (`rewrite_futile`), and `arc_phase` is recorded in the per-tick coherence rubric. Alongside this, a new design doc (`docs/BLOCKS_CONTRACTS_LANDING_SKETCH.md`) reconciles the 2025 blocks/contracts architecture proposal with what has actually shipped, cutting the remaining work into slices: contracts riding on plot beats as the MVP, then precondition pressure, bounded contract repair, scene skeletons, and a measured per-sub-block generation experiment.

The sketch's first slices did not stay on paper long. The same day, beat generation itself was taught the curve: each beat in a generated batch is scheduled at its future tick, given an interpolated tension target and a phase directive in the generation prompt, and authored targets are reconciled to the schedule after parsing (clamp to a plus or minus 2 band, fill when missing). On top of that, contracts Slice 1 landed: `PlotBeat` now carries pre/postconditions authored at beat-generation time from the closed checker vocabulary, sanitized in both generation paths (unknown checks and phantom refs dropped, tension conditions reconciled against the beat's own target), evaluated during beat verification (a pass upgrades confidence, a failure routes to the existing keep-pending and horizon-revision paths), and measured in the coherence rubric. The stale `contracts.json` store and `ContractManager` are retired. A validation re-run of the June descent scenario confirmed the mandate works where it matters: the final scene dropped from 8/10 to 6/10 against a target of 4, with the planner demonstrably choosing aftermath events instead of confrontations (`docs/progress_report_20260709.md`).

**Decisions & notes:** The mandate is gated by `coherence.arc_phase_mandate` (default True; False restores exact pre-mandate behavior for A/B runs), but the rubric records `arc_phase` ungated so on/off runs stay comparable. The mandate's known gap at commit time (plot beat generation not receiving the phase) was closed the same day by the beat-generation bridge. Contracts stay behind `generation.use_contracts` (default False) until a plot-first smoke run exercises them live. On contracts, the sanitizer enforces consistency with the beat's tension rather than prohibiting contracts from authoring tension conditions. Two validation findings worth carrying forward: the rising phase ran hotter than the June control (possibly the escalate mandate amplifying the genre's hot bias), and the resolution mandate's close-open-loops clause did not bite (8 loops opened, 0 closed), which is direct evidence for the planned loop-aging pressure.
